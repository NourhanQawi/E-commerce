## E-commerce Profitability & Delivery Performance Analysis

## Business Problem
Despite strong sales volumes, the e-commerce business experienced unstable profitability.
Management observed frequent delivery delays and order cancellations, raising concerns about
profit leakage and operational inefficiencies.

## Dataset Overview
Transactional e-commerce dataset containing:
- Orders across Office Supplies, Furniture, and Technology
- Customer type (New vs Repeat)
- Delivery status (On-time, Late, Canceled, Advance)
- Sales, profit, quantity, and discounts
- Geographic coverage across all U.S. regions

## Tools Used
- Excel: Data cleaning, pivot tables, exploratory analysis
- Business analysis techniques for segmentation and diagnosis

## Analytical Approach
The analysis followed a diagnostic, business-driven approach:
- Segmented customers into new and repeat groups
- Analyzed profitability by delivery status
- Identified loss concentration by category and region
- Compared sales volume versus profit contribution

## Key Insights
- High sales volume did not guarantee profitability
- Delivery cancellations consistently resulted in negative profit
- Repeat customers provided more stable value but were sensitive to service failures
- Profitability issues were concentrated in specific regions and product categories

## Recommendations
- Prioritize reducing delivery cancellations, especially for high-volume products
- Review shipping partners and logistics performance in underperforming regions
- Protect repeat customers from service disruptions
- Reassess pricing and discount strategies for low-margin items

## Outcome
The analysis provided clear, actionable insights to support operational improvements
and more predictable profitability.
